Smartphone Buying Guide



A quick round-up of the points you should keep in mind while buying a smartphone:

PERFORMANCE

  • Processor :- Offers speed and power efficiency. You can enjoy better performance with higher cores and higher clock speed in GHz.
  • RAM :- Higher RAM offers a smoother multitasking experience. The speed of the processor primarily depends on its size. Smaller processors enable faster data transfer, thereby making the processor more battery efficient.

STORAGE

The number of videos, photos and other data that can be stored on your phone depends on its storage space. Higher the storage space, higher the number of videos, photos etc.
PRO TIP: Your phone usually provides only 75% of Primary Storage as some space is assigned for Internal Memory and Pre-installed applications.

DISPLAY

Higher Resolution, Pixel Per Inch and Refresh Rate allows you to experience a more beautiful, colorful and clearer display. Your phone will consume even more battery if the resolution, PPI and Refresh Rate is high.

How to choose the perfect display for you?

  • Analyse the type and quantity of media you use in a day on your phone
  • Do you use your phone for gaming?
  • Understand and evaluate how much you use your phone to edit photos and videos

BATTERY LIFE

Your complaints of poor battery life will only end if you check the battery capacity before buying a smartphone. CAMERA

  • The camera's aperture, set up and pixel size determines its performance and quality.
  • Mega pixel does not qualify your camera as a good one. It is defined by how much data the camera can capture in one shot.
  • Despite a 12 Megapixel shooter, an iPhone does an incredible job against the 48 megapixel cameras in the market.
  • The quality of your photo significantly depends on the Image processing power of your phone.
  • You should pay special attention to Optical Image stabilization if you take a lot of videos.
  • Multiple Cameras in a phone does not necessarily mean that your camera is mind-blowing.
