Microwave Oven Buying Guide



Microwave Ovens have introduced us to comfort and ease in our kitchens with their multifunctional nature. Whether it’s reheating leftover pizza, preparing a fresh meal or getting some popcorn ready for the movie night, it does it all in no time.

TYPES OF MICROWAVE OVENS

CAPACITY

EXCITING FEATURES

  • AUTO COOK MENU :- The Auto-Cook Menu is designed to help you select the food you want to cook from a list of predefined recipes. This setting will automatically set the time and power level for your food to ensure that your food is not undercooked or overcooked.
  • CHILD LOCK :- As the feature suggests, Child Lock helps you disable the microwave so that your child cannot fidget with the microwave’s functions and features.
  • ROTISSERIE :- Rotisserie is a grilling accessory that comes with a few microwave ovens. You can use it for grilling and barbecuing vegetables, paneer, tofu and meat.
  • DEFROST :- The defrost function will help you unfreeze your food quickly. Auto-defrost is easy to understand & use: add the frozen food’s weight and the oven will set the best program for it. You can also do this manually by setting the power and time yourself.
  • PREHEAT :- Preheating means getting your oven heated to a particular temperature before you put in what you want to prepare. It’s usually used for preparing cakes, cookies, and certain food items that require the grill function.
  • TIMER :- Timer allows you to choose a timeframe for your heating or cooking program. A bell rings to indicate that your program has been completed successfully.

PANEL TYPES

A good control panel makes your microwave oven easy to use and operate. From setting the timer to selecting a preset function, you can access all the functions and features just at the push of a button.

  • Remember to clean the interior and exteriors (including the door seal) after use.
  • Always use oven mitts while using a microwave as vessels become hot in it.
  • Do not allow kids to come near or operate the microwave.
  • Do not put the microwave on or operate it when it is empty.
  • Use only microwave safe cookware like glass, ceramics, plastic and paper.
  • Do not use metallic cookware or vessels with metallic edges inside the microwave.
